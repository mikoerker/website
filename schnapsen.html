Evaluating Search Depth in Schnapsen: A Minimax vs. Alpha-Beta Showdown

Associated with Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam

In the Intelligent Systems course, I developed and evaluated intelligent agents to play the Austrian card game Schnapsen. The project involved implementing and analyzing advanced AI algorithms, including Minimax and Alpha-Beta pruning, to optimize decision-making in competitive game scenarios. I conducted extensive experiments to assess the impact of search depth on algorithmic performance, focusing on win rates, heuristic scores, and computational efficiency. The project also included the creation of custom bots with depth-limited search trees and heuristic functions to guide decision-making. Through this work, I enhanced my skills in adversarial search techniques, heuristic evaluation, and performance analysis, while contributing to a deeper understanding of AI strategies in game theory.

Skills: Python, Algorithms, Performance Analysis, Data Analysis, Adversarial Search Techniques, Problem Solving, Game Theory, Heuristic Evaluation, Experimental Design
